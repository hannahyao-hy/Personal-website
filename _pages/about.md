I'm Hannah, a banker by day, builder and researcher by night. My work sits at the intersection of capital markets and emerging technology, and I'm especially drawn to how AI is reshaping the way we model risk, communicate ideas, and make decisions.

Before J.P. Morgan, I studied at Northwestern, where I picked up a love for both rigorous analysis and the messy parts of building things from scratch. This site is where I keep my research, projects, and occasional notes.

My current interests include:

- AI applications in finance, capital markets, and risk modeling
- machine learning systems that make complex decisions easier to reason about
- writing, prototypes, and research notes that connect technical ideas to real-world workflows

I use this site as a living notebook: part portfolio, part research archive, and part scratchpad for ideas that are still taking shape.
